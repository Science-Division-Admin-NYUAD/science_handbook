##### Life in Abu Dhabi

## About


The United Arab Emirates (UAE) is a federation of seven emirates: Abu Dhabi, Dubai, Sharjah, Ajman, Umm Al Quwain, Ras Al Khaimah, and Fujairah. Meaning "Land of the Gazelle" in Arabic, according to legend, the emirate of Abu Dhabi was established when a young antelope led a nomadic tribe to a freshwater source on an island that initially had just 300 Barasti (palm frond) huts, a few coral buildings, and the Ruler's fort. This modest island settlement has since evolved into the modern, diverse, and internationally renowned capital city of the United Arab Emirates.

Although NYUAD is an international community, we should be conscious of local customs and cultural norms here in the UAE. Accordingly, please refer to the Intranet list of particular things to be mindful of while in the UAE.

#### Working Week and Weekend
The work and school week in Abu Dhabi is Monday through Friday, with the weekend falling on Saturday and Sunday. Schools and Abu Dhabi government companies work a half-day on Fridays, allowing the afternoon free for prayer. NYUAD maintains a full working day on Friday; however, note that 12:00-2:20pm is prayer time and thus no work, meetings, or events should be scheduled to occur during this period.

#### Official Language
Although Arabic is the official language of the UAE, English is widely spoken by the general population. All signs, including road signs, stores, and restaurants, are either in English or both English and Arabic. Most companies employ multilingual staff or crew who are conversant in many languages.

## Things to Do in Abu Dhabi

#### Saadiyat Island: A Cultural District on the Doorstep
Located on Saadiyat Island, a place dedicated to innovation and creativity, research and knowledge, NYUAD is within close proximity to Louvre Abu Dhabi and Manarat Al Saadiyat, as well as some of the country's most beautiful beaches and an abundance of incredible wildlife.

#### Manarat Al Saadiyat
Meaning "a place of enlightenment" in Arabic, Manarat Al Saadiyat is Abu Dhabi's art, cultural activity, and community events center that comprises an outdoor events terrace, cafe and brasserie, three galleries for temporary exhibits, a photography studio, an art studio, 250-seat auditorium, and gift shop.

#### Abrahamic Family House
Encompassing a mosque, a church, a synagogue and a Forum for gathering and dialogue, the Abrahamic Family House welcomes people from all walks of life to exchange knowledge and practice faith. It is dedicated to the pursuit of peaceful coexistence for generations to come.

#### Jubail Mangrove Park
Nestled between Saadiyat Island and Yas Island lies Jubail Mangrove Park, a sanctuary where you can explore a haven of avian and marine species native to Abu Dhabi. A meandering boardwalk takes you through the mangroves and allows you to better understand and appreciate the important ecological function of the mangrove habitats that protect the capital's coastline and support biodiversity.

#### Louvre Abu Dhabi
The first universal museum in the Arab World, Louvre Abu Dhabi fosters a spirit of openness by celebrating connections between civilizations and cultures. The museum houses works of historical, cultural and sociological significance from ancient times to the contemporary era, within the walls of its dazzling architecture that combines French design with Arabic heritage.

#### Mamsha Al Saadiyat
With the glistening Soul Beach on one side, Mamsha Al Saadiyat is a vibrant beachfront promenade offering an eclectic mix of restaurants and cafes, serving local and international cuisine. You can stop for a bite to eat, drop by for a coffee or a cocktail, or simply take a stroll along the beachwalk to take in the beautiful views and bustling atmosphere.

#### Saadiyat Hotels
Saadiyat Island is home to numerous luxury hotels that offer an array of exquisite restaurants and bars, providing visitors with an unforgettable dining experience. These establishments are perfect for enjoying an a la carte meal or refreshing drinks while taking in the breathtaking ocean and sunset views. From elegant fine dining venues to relaxed beachside bars, each spot offers a unique ambience and culinary delights that cater to diverse tastes. Non-hotel guests are welcome; however, advance reservation is recommended. Some hotel options include Jumeirah Saadiyat Island, Park Hyatt Abu Dhabi, Saadiyat Rotana Resort & Villas, and The St. Regis Saadiyat Island Resort.

#### Berklee Abu Dhabi
Next door to Manarat, a striking dune-shaped structure designed by the renowned London-based architects Foster + Partners houses the prestigious Berklee Abu Dhabi. This marks the first Middle Eastern outpost of Berklee College of Music, the pre-eminent institute of contemporary music and the performing arts. Berklee Abu Dhabi offers world-class educational experiences through workshops, masterclasses, courses, ensembles, and labs, all led by world-renowned artists and instructors. Originally, the structure was the UAE Pavilion at the World Expo 2010 in Shanghai, China, where it greeted nearly two million visitors before being meticulously relocated, piece by piece.

#### Saadiyat Wildlife
Spanning a total area of 59 square kilometers, the pristine beaches and marine area of Saadiyat form the protected Saadiyat Marine National Park. There is an abundance of wildlife to be found here, including the endangered Hawksbill turtles that nest on the island and the graceful free-roaming gazelles that can often be spotted in the grasslands around Saadiyat Beach Golf Club. Dolphins are also seen jumping in the turquoise waters, against the backdrop of Saadiyat's stunning sunsets.

## Across the Emirate: Things to See and Do

#### Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque
Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque is an architectural marvel that can house up to 55,000 worshippers and visitors every day and welcomes people of all beliefs. The mosque is one of the world's largest and was the vision of Sheikh Zayed bin Sultan Al Nahyan, the Founding Father of the UAE. He envisaged the creation of a welcoming and cultural haven that inspires people from all backgrounds. Even the construction was a cultural collaboration. The grand structure was designed and built by skilled teams from all over the world, including 1,300 Iranian artisans who hand-knotted the main prayer hall's carpet, the world's largest. Mosques hold a religious and sacred position for all Muslims. Visitors to mosques should therefore dress conservatively and behave respectfully, according to local guidelines.

#### Qasr Al Hosn Fort
Qasr Al Hosn Fort is a historical landmark and the oldest stone building in the city of Abu Dhabi. Built in 1761, it is also known as the White Fort or the Old Fort. Originally constructed as a conical watchtower to protect the Bani Yas Settlement, it was expanded to a small fort and later became the ancestral home of the Royal Al Nahyan family. It now stands as a national monument that encapsulates the development of the city from a modest island settlement into a modern, cosmopolitan capital city renowned for its diverse culture, architectural marvels, and rapid economic growth. The dress code at Qasr Al Hosn Fort is not as strict as the Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque; however, it is considered a culturally significant part of the city and it is therefore recommended that you dress conservatively, wearing trousers and covering your shoulders, with no tight or revealing clothing.

#### Abu Dhabi Corniche
Abu Dhabi's Corniche Road stretches across an impressive eight kilometers of manicured waterfront that includes children's play areas, separate cycle and pedestrian pathways, cafes and restaurants, and the stunning, lifeguarded Corniche Beach. If you are looking to soak up Abu Dhabi's famous year-round sunshine, this immaculate beach is one of the best places to do it. From the Corniche, Qasr Al Hosn, Emirates Palace Mandarin Oriental Hotel, and the Observation Deck at 300 are all accessible by a short taxi ride.

#### Qasr Al Watan
The presidential palace of the United Arab Emirates, Qasr Al Watan was first opened to the public in 2019. A majestic palace, which translates to "Palace of the Nation," its intricate design features stunning white domes, ornate carvings, and lavish interiors, making it not only a center of governance but also a testament to the nation's artistic and cultural achievements. The palace is used for official purposes, such as hosting foreign Heads of State, and for meetings of the country's Supreme Council and Federal Cabinet. Visitors can explore its magnificent halls, witness cultural performances, and enjoy the beautifully landscaped gardens, all while gaining a deeper understanding of the UAE's journey and vision for the future. As a courtesy to other guests in the Palace and in accordance with local laws and customs, guests are asked to wear respectful clothing, with knees and shoulders covered.

#### Emirates Palace Mandarin Oriental Hotel
A stunning architectural marvel and one of Abu Dhabi's iconic landmarks, the main building of the Emirates Palace Mandarin Oriental Hotel spans over a kilometer from one wing to the other, with gardens extending across one hundred hectares. The palace features 114 domes, with the central dome standing an impressive 72.6 meters above ground, intricately adorned with gold, mother-of-pearl, and crystals. Additionally, the hotel is home to 1,002 chandeliers, the largest of which weighs 2.5 tons.

#### Observation Deck at 300
Situated in the second tower of Etihad Towers, on the 74th floor of Conrad Abu Dhabi Etihad Towers, the Observation Deck at 300 offers the highest vantage point in Abu Dhabi. From 300 meters above the UAE capital, visitors can enjoy unparalleled, bird's-eye views of the city skyline and the Arabian Gulf. The Observation Deck's cafe is renowned for its afternoon high tea, which features an impressive selection of tea blends and freshly-ground coffee, accompanied by an assortment of delectable sweet and savory treats.

#### Yas Island
A dedicated leisure destination, Yas Island is home to the iconic Yas Marina Circuit, several theme parks, prestigious golf clubs, and the expansive Yas Mall, making it an all-encompassing destination for entertainment and recreation. Yas Marina Circuit, famous for hosting the annual Formula 1 Etihad Airways Abu Dhabi Grand Prix, offers high-speed excitement for motorsport enthusiasts. Visitors can indulge in the thrills of Ferrari World, Yas Waterworld, and Warner Bros. World Abu Dhabi, each offering a range of rides and attractions.

##### Communication, Activity Reporting, and Promoting Your Research

## Communication


### Newsletters

#### Institutional Newsletters
NYUAD distributes newsletters for staff (Al Sho'la) and faculty (Faculty Gazette) that offer updates on upcoming events, important dates and deadlines, announcements, tailored professional development and funding opportunities, NYU global news, and other relevant group information. New employees are automatically subscribed to receive these newsletters.

#### Science Division Newsletter
The monthly Science Division Newsletter delivers updates on the latest news, publications, and upcoming events relevant to the Division of Science. Content is primarily sourced from the Tamkeen Monthly Report (see below). New employees to the Division of Science are automatically subscribed. If you have feedback or contributions, contact Katie Stanworth at kls10@nyu.edu.

#### Daily and Weekly Announcements
You can expect to receive daily and weekly announcements summarizing divisional events, research talks, and other activities from the Science and Engineering Divisions. You will be automatically subscribed to these communications.

#### Intranet Announcements
You are also advised to regularly check the NYUAD Intranet homepage for University-wide announcements and upcoming events.

### Calendars and Email Groups

#### Events Calendar
The Science Events Calendar is kept up to date with all program seminars and events related to the Division of Science, in addition to cross-divisional activities such as the Science Open Houses and Capstone Festival. Your program may also use additional calendars to schedule faculty meetings and log program teaching schedules. Your program administrators will share these with you.

#### Division of Science General
As well as CRB or ERB Distribution Lists, based on your primary scientific affiliation you will be added to one of the following email groups, where you can expect to receive the daily and weekly announcement emails:

- CRB and ERB Distribution: erb-distribution-list-group@nyu.edu · crb-distribution-list-group@nyu.edu
- Science Faculty: science-faculty-group@nyu.edu — all science faculty are automatically added and will receive information from the Dean's Office.
- Researcher Group: erb-researchers-group@nyu.edu — ERB researchers and staff can use this to share and communicate lab-based research questions.

To share announcements via these emails, send the information to your Program Administrative Coordinator, or directly to erb.crb.announcements@nyu.edu.

## Activity Reporting


#### Faculty 180
Each year, all NYU faculty from all of the NYU campuses report on their activities: teaching, research, and service. NYU uses this centralized system to generate reports, celebrate faculty successes and awards, and to minimize the information requests we send to faculty. You will submit your report using Interfolio Faculty 180 (F180). Entries are used to create research reports for the Division, and for use in the APR and accreditation documents. You will receive an email copy of your submissions which you can file for future reference.

#### Tamkeen Monthly Highlights
Each month, faculty will receive an email asking them to report on any highlights or activities from that month. This process is very important: it is a central request. The Office of Public Affairs and External Engagement generates a consolidated report for our investors, Tamkeen, featuring highlights, research activity, and accomplishments from the whole University. Content from the report is extracted for marketing and communication purposes, and featured across our communication channels, including the website, the monthly newsletter, social media, and — in applicable cases — the local/international media.

#### Press Releases
If you have a particular highlight or achievement that you think may be newsworthy (for example, something of regional or societal relevance; a discovery; an award; an impressive publication; a new grant), please inform Katie Stanworth with as much notice as possible. In the case of an upcoming paper, you must get in touch in advance of publication so the material can be reviewed confidentially, under embargo. A press release will then be drafted to coincide with the paper's release. Once published, you should also report it in the Tamkeen Monthly Highlights and upload to Faculty 180. For more information or to share any news, email Katie Stanworth — kls10@nyu.edu.
